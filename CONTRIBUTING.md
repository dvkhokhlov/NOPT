# Contributing to NOPT

Thank you for your interest in contributing to NOPT.

## License of Contributions ("Inbound = Outbound")

NOPT is distributed under the MIT License (see [LICENSE](LICENSE)).

By submitting a contribution to this project — whether a patch, pull
request, issue containing code, documentation change, or any other form of
material — you agree that your contribution is licensed to the project and
to its users under the same MIT License that covers the project as a whole.

This "inbound = outbound" rule means that the license you grant for your
contribution is identical to the license under which the project is
distributed. It is the convention used by a large share of open-source
projects (including, for example, the policy followed by the Linux kernel
and adopted by GitHub's default terms of service) and is sufficient on its
own: **no separate Contributor License Agreement (CLA) is required to
contribute to NOPT**.

By submitting a contribution, you also confirm that:

- The contribution is your original work, or you otherwise have the right
  to submit it under the MIT License (for example, with the permission of
  the rights holder).
- Your contribution does not introduce third-party code under a license
  incompatible with MIT without prior discussion with the maintainers.

## Third-Party Code

If a contribution adds or updates a third-party dependency, please update
[THIRD_PARTY_NOTICES.md](THIRD_PARTY_NOTICES.md) accordingly and place the
corresponding upstream license text under `licenses/<component>/`. The
license of the new dependency must be compatible with MIT; see the
compatibility table in `THIRD_PARTY_NOTICES.md`.

## Reporting Issues

Please use the project issue tracker for bug reports, feature requests,
and questions about the codebase.
